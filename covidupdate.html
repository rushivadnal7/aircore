COVID-19 UPDATES

The guidelines for domestic and international flights in India amid the COVID-19 pandemic were as follows:

Domestic Flights:


Throughout the entire journey, passengers are required to wear masks at all times. Before entering the departures hall at the airport, passengers will be checked for symptoms including fever, coughing, and shortness of breath. Only those passengers who are not showing any COVID-19 symptoms will be permitted to board the plane. To minimize interaction at the airport, passengers are being encouraged to use online check-in services. Carrying hand sanitizers and upholding social distance standards are encouraged for passengers.

International Flights:

At least 72 hours before to their scheduled flight, passengers must complete self-declaration forms using the airport's web portal. A unfavorable RT-PCR test result obtained within 72 hours of the trip is required of all passengers. Upon arriving at the airport, passengers must go through a thermal screening procedure. It is recommended that passengers download the Aarogya Setu app to their smartphones. Upon arrival, passengers are subject to a 14-day institutional quarantine followed by a 7-day home quarantine. However, given that the situation could have changed since the cutoff date for my knowledge, I would advise consulting the relevant authorities to confirm the most recent regulations.
